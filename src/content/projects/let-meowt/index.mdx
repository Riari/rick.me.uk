**Let Meowt** is my submission to Godot Wild Jam #95. The theme for this jam was "Retro", and the game is a 90s-inspired platformer in which you play as a cat wreaking havoc!

I teamed up with <ExtLink src="https://www.linkedin.com/in/dianna-koh-yy/">Dianna Koh</ExtLink> (who did most of the art) and <ExtLink src="https://terra.me.uk/">Matt Ellis</ExtLink> (who contributed to the code) to develop the game. The core idea was to turn <ExtLink src="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o6QPOUgbS6c">cats' general obsession with knocking things over</ExtLink> into a central gameplay mechanic.

Features I implemented include:

* Fixed 320x180 resolution and a limited 16-colour palette to achieve a late 80s / early 90s look and feel
* A component system for composing character behaviours, including components for movement, combat, and animation
* Enemy AI with player seeking and platform detection for jumping/dropping
* A custom player camera with smooth following
* A player controller with the ability to Meow and Loaf
* Music for the main menu and win screen

Beyond these systems, I also handled most of the other development tasks, such as integrating artwork, setting up animation frames, designing levels, implementing collision, and creating a UI theme that works within the limited screen resolution.

You can <ExtLink url="https://riari89.itch.io/let-meowt">play it in the browser on itch.io</ExtLink>.
